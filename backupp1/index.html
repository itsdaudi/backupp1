About Me

For me, I believe photography is more than taking pictures. It's about capturing moments, emotions, and stories that would otherwise be lost to time. Every click of the shutter is an opportunity to preserve a piece of history.

My journey began over a decade ago when I picked up my first camera. Since then, I've traveled across the country, documenting people, cultures, and landscapes that tell the story of our shared human experience.

When I'm not behind the lens, you can find me playing football, swimming at the beaches, or doing a little bit of dancing. These activities keep me energized and inspired for my next photographic adventure.

My Journey

2017

The Beginning

Started my photography journey with a simple point-and-shoot camera, inspired by Billy Miaron's documentary work.

2020

First Exhibition

Participated in a local group exhibition titled "Moments in Time," which featured my early street photography work.

2023

Full-Time Focus

Became a full-time photographer, focusing on documentary and portrait photography for NGOs and publications.

2024

International Recognition

Won the "Moment Collector" award at the African Photography Awards for my series on coastal communities.

2026 - Present

Mentorship & Workshops

Started conducting photography workshops and mentoring young photographers, continuing the cycle of inspiration.
